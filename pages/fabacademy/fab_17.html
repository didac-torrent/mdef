/application_&_implications

(24_may_2022)

This session of FabAcademy developed the topic of applications and implications. During the FabAcademy we've been developing mini projects and independent tasks in order to practice and learn about digital fabrication and prototyping technologies. However, we haven't talked that much about the applications of these technologies in real problems or situations.

Through this session, we could see some real projects developed during FabAcademy in other places and years, such as the FabLab House made by some FabLab alumni.

.reflecting_on_applications



To begin with, I wanted to comment that the project I'm developing is not a single machine or product, a thing that makes it a bit difficult to answer some of the questions proposed for this task. My project, SYNE, gathers different devices related to environmental sensing and experimentation with senses. The aim is to collect tools to democratise sensing, empathising with other realities and perceiving more from what is around us.

Some projects have been developed around this topic, but it is hard to find one that gathers devices like the ones we created, that don't have a specific user but can be used by anyone in almost any context, always having the experimentation with senses as the connection point.

All the tools created until now can be replicated and fabricated by anyone who has a 3D printer or a FabLab that has it close to them. They also require some controller such as an ESP32, ESP8266 or Arduino with a Bluetooth module and the sensors for each device.





The cost of it would be different for each tool, and it would also depend on the price of the materials at each moment. For instance, microcontrollers now are more expensive than before because there's a lack of components. At the same time, 3D printing technologies and materials are everytime cheaper and more accessible to everyone.

Since this is more of an experimental project, it doesn't need to answer a specific question. It's more about playing, feeling and empathising using our senses. Some of the applications for tools would be:

- Schools: using the tools to make kids empathise with other realities or to practice their capabilities of sensing.

- Workshops: use the devices as a method to run other activites.

- Art exhibitions: some tools can be used to perform exhibitions and to add value to it. They can add sound and visuals.
